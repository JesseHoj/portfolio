## Work Experience
With 14 years of experience in internal technology at an international financial services firm, I developed a strong foundation in data-informed decision-making and technology-enabled collaboration. I served as product owner for several critical applications supporting 40,000 clients and 4,000 staff, where I strengthened my ability to translate complex data into actionable insights, communicate effectively with diverse stakeholders, and work cross-functionally to address organizational challenges. In late 2025, I transitioned to full-time academic study, expanding my training in data science, R, and applied machine learning. My long-term goal is to pursue full-time research examining how executives, managers, and workers construct competing meanings of AI in organizational life, and how some of those meanings become institutionally legitimate while others are marginalized. I hope to draw on Bourdieu’s theory of symbolic power and Jasanoff’s concept of sociotechnical imaginaries to study how elite discourse frames AI as desirable, inevitable, and authoritative, and how those framings shape labor relations, worker agency, and the reproduction of inequality.
